Welcome to the personal webpage of Ken Duncan. I'm a PhD student at the University of Nottingham working under the supervision of Professor Chris Conselice as a part of the CANDELS collaboration. My research interests are on the growth and evolution of galaxies during the first 2 billion years of the universe and the role of galaxies in the epoch of reionization. My current active research includes:

  • The galaxy stellar mass function and star formation rates at \(z > 4 \) in the CANDELS GOODS South field
  • Estimating the major merger rates of galaxies at \(z > 3 \) in the full CANDELS survey using close-pair statistics and morphological tracers
  • Using SED modelling to better understand the constraints placed by our current observations on the ionizing emissivity of high redshift galaxies
